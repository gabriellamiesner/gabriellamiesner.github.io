gabriella miesner


Gabriella is an 18 year old student at the University of Missouri, majoring in Information Technology, with a certificate in Web and Mobile Applications Development. She is involved with the Mizzou Computing Association, where she helped to rebuild the website as a part of the Web Development Special Interest Group. Gabriella also leads the Data Science Special Interest Group, where she helps new data scientists learn the neccesary skills to analyze data and creates data visualizations with her team.

She loves to create things with code, but how she fell into the wonderful world of technology is a bit atypical. When Gabriella was an avid reader in the sixth grade, she wanted to connect with other readers and talk about all the books she was reading. So she created a book blog, where she did connect with other readers and talk about books, but she also began to learn the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Ever since then, Gabriella has been learning and creating things with code in some capacity, even if the road there has been a little bit bumpy.

Gabriella was a 2020 Web Development Scholar and a 2019 Mobile Development Scholar.

Gabriella was a marketing organizer for the 2021 TechTogether Seattle Hackathon and was a brand ambassador for the 2020-2021 TechTogether hackathon season.

Gabriella is a graduate of the July 2019 Java CoderGirl cohort, where she learned Java and JavaScript. She currently serves as a TA, teaching Java and JavaScript to LC101 students.

Gabriella was a 2020 Computer Science Summer Institute Scholar, where she learned p5.js.

Gabriella was a marketing organizer for the 2021 PickHacks Hackathon, where she ran the instagram and created social media content leading up to the hackathon.
